8. Memory system for AI agents to survive context compaction

Hacker News
https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=46826485
Context compaction is brutal — you lose architectural decisions, rejected approaches, and the reasoning behind your constraints. I built SpecLock to solve this differently. Instead of trying to preserve everything through compaction, it externalizes the critical stuff: - Goals, locks (non-negotiable constraints), and decisions stored in .speclock/brain.json - Session briefings reload full context at conversation start - Locks are embedded in package.json so the AI sees them even in fresh sessions - Protected files get SPECLOCK-GUARD headers the AI reads before editing The enforcement layer is the key part — even if the AI "forgets" a constraint after compaction, the lock still exists and conflict detection catches violations. Works as MCP server or npm CLI. Free, open source: https://github.com/sgroy10/speclock
