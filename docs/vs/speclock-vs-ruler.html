SPECLOCK vs RULER

SpecLock vs Ruler: sync your AI rules, or enforce them?

Ruler keeps one set of rules consistent across every AI agent. SpecLock makes those rules unbreakable at runtime. Here's an honest look at where they overlap — and where SpecLock adds the layer Ruler was never built to provide.

Ruler (by intellectronica, ~2.7k stars) is the clear category leader for rule synchronization. You write your AI instructions once in a central .ruler/ directory, and Ruler distributes them into the native formats each agent expects — GitHub Copilot, Claude Code, Cursor, and more. It solves a real, annoying problem: keeping the same guidance in sync when every tool wants its own rules file.

But Ruler is honest about what it is: a distribution tool. It puts the same rules in front of every agent. What it does not do — and doesn't claim to — is stop the AI from ignoring them. Once the rules are synced, the model is still free to forget, dilute, or override any line, exactly as it would with a single hand-maintained rules file.

That's the gap SpecLock fills. SpecLock reads the same rules file you already wrote (CLAUDE.md, .cursorrules, AGENTS.md) and compiles its prose into runtime hard constraints — locks — that block conflicting changes before they reach your codebase, across Claude Code, Cursor, Windsurf, Cline, and Copilot.

When to use which

This isn't strictly an either/or. Ruler and SpecLock solve adjacent problems, and you can run them together.

Use Ruler if…

Your main pain is fragmentation — you maintain the same guidance in five different rules files and they keep drifting apart. Ruler's central .ruler/ source of truth and fan-out to native formats is purpose-built for that, and it's the most mature option in the sync category.

Use SpecLock if…

Your pain is that the AI ignores the rules even when it can see them — it touches files you told it never to touch, or quietly refactors locked architecture. Syncing the same rule to more agents doesn't fix that; only enforcement does. SpecLock turns the rule into a hard stop and hands you a receipt of every block.

Use both if…

You want one canonical rule set and teeth behind it. Let Ruler keep your rules consistent across every agent; let SpecLock enforce the ones that must never be broken. They're complementary — Ruler is the distribution layer, SpecLock is the enforcement layer.

Ruler distributes. SpecLock enforces.

Ruler is excellent at making your rules consistent everywhere. But consistent rules the AI can still ignore are still just suggestions. SpecLock is the layer that makes them stick — one command, no new files to write.
